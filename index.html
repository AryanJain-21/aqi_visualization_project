US Air Quality Index (AQI) Analysis

Introduction

The Air Quality Index (AQI) provides a measure of air pollution levels and their potential health effects. This analysis explores AQI trends across the United States over time, examining regional, seasonal, and categorical patterns. Additionally, we investigate correlations with weather factors and population data.

Data Sources

  • EPA AQI Data: Annual AQI reports for US states and counties.
  • Natural Earth Geospatial Data: State and county shapefiles for mapping.

Visualizations

Seasonal Trends in AQI

This visualization highlights the average AQI by season for each state. Peaks in certain seasons may indicate increased pollution due to specific activities or natural events.

AQI Trends

Understanding the distribution of AQI categories (Good, Moderate, Unhealthy) across states and years reveals regions with consistently poor air quality.

Geospatial Patterns

This map shows the average AQI by state, highlighting regional disparities in air quality.

Correlation with Weather Factors

This heatmap illustrates correlations between AQI and weather variables like temperature, humidity, and wind speed.

Conclusion

This analysis provides insights into air quality trends, regional disparities, and potential influencing factors. Further work could include time series analysis and exploring health impacts of AQI variations.
